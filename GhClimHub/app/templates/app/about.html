Know Us

 

Did you know that the whole of West Africa is a drought prone area?

Did you also know that Rainfall variability, driven by global and regional climatic processes accounted for the major  droughts in 1982/83, 1992, 1997, 2001, 2007 and 2015/2016? This eventually resulted in adverse socio-economic impacts on the nation at large. Nothing much could be done to prevent it due to the absence of and/or lack of reliable high resolution hydro-meteorological data.

Africa at large also has no such platform to monitor drought occurrences and predict the next likely drought zone. This challenge was thrown to us to develop a high resolution online National Drought Observatory, which will incorporate satellite data to monitor Rainfall, Vegetation Health and Plant Water stress to serve as an early drought warning system (EDWS).

This will be achieved with the aid of the Google Earth Engine platform which provides a multi-petabyte catalogue of satellite data with global-scale analysis capabilities to allow land surface and hydro-meterological monitoring.

We have heard the cries of the farmers and the society at large. No more famine! No more limited food supply! No more heat-related illnesses, waterborne and cardiorespiratory illnesses!

The farmer will be happy again, society will smile again, the nation at large will progress and move forward....Forward ever, backwards never.
